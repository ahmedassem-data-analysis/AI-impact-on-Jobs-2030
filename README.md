# AI Impact on Jobs 2030

## Project Overview

This project analyzes the potential impact of artificial intelligence and automation on different job roles by 2030. The analysis focuses on understanding which occupations may face higher automation risk, which roles are more resilient, and how factors such as AI exposure, technology growth, education level, salary range, and years of experience relate to future job security.

The project was built using Excel, pivot tables, KPI calculations, slicers, and dashboard visuals to transform raw job-market data into clear business insights.

## Key Objectives

* Analyze automation probability across different job titles.
* Identify high-risk, medium-risk, and low-risk occupations.
* Compare AI exposure levels with automation risk.
* Explore the relationship between experience, education level, salary range, and job automation.
* Build an interactive dashboard that supports quick decision-making and trend analysis.

## Dataset

The dataset includes job-related fields such as:

* Job Title
* Average Salary
* Years of Experience
* Education Level
* AI Exposure Index
* Technology Growth Factor
* Automation Probability by 2030
* Risk Category

## Dashboard Features

The dashboard includes:

* Overall automation probability KPI
* High, medium, and low-risk job distribution
* Most at-risk occupation
* Most resilient occupation
* Automation prediction by job title
* Average yearly salary comparison
* Salary range by risk category
* Experience band and automation relationship
* Interactive filters for job title, risk category, education level, and experience band

## Key Insights

* The overall automation probability is around 50%, showing that AI may have a major impact on the future job market.
* Medium-risk jobs represent the largest share of the dataset.
* Retail Worker appears as one of the most at-risk occupations.
* AI Engineer appears as one of the most resilient occupations.
* Jobs with higher exposure to AI do not always have the highest automation probability, meaning job risk depends on multiple factors, not AI exposure alone.

## Conclusion

This project demonstrates how data analysis can be used to assess future workforce trends and automation risk. The dashboard provides a clear view of how AI may affect different occupations by 2030 and helps users identify which job categories may require reskilling, upskilling, or strategic career planning.
